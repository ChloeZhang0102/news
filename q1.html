ANIMALS ARE LOVIN' IT
MCPLANT
BACKGROUND

McDonald’s Australia has launched the McPlant burger in select stores as part of a new trial. It is

plant based and made from Beyond Meat, making it suitable for vegetarians.

PROBLEM & CHALLENGE

​McDonald's was late in introducing meat-free burgers and may not be the best 'meat-free' option

for Australians, especially vegans.

PURPOSE

Increase McPlant's exposure and get on consumers' meat-free food lists, or even first choice.

TARGET AUDIENCE
Australians with reduced meat consumption (including Vegan).
INSIGHT

Animal welfare is one of the main reasons why more and more Australians are consciously

reducing their meat consumption (choosing to eat less meat, or even no meat at all).

Of these, 57% of Australians believe that eating plant-based food is animal friendly.

BIG IDEA
The burger that animals want to eat - plant-based meat, no harm, no guilty.
POSTER IDEATION

The use of animal images of common animal meat (cow, chicken, fish) as part of the poster

demonstrates McPlant's animal-friendliness by highlighting their desire and liking for McPlant,

thus proving that McPlant is truly a guilt-free, meat-free burger.

TAGLINE
Not ridiculous at all. It's just eating MCPLANT.

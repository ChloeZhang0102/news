NO ALCOHOL DURING PREGNANCY CAMPAIGN
BACKGROUND

Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD) is a diagnostic term used to describe impacts on the brain

and body of individuals prenatally exposed to alcohol. FASD is a lifelong disability and is still not

widely understood in Australia, and many myths exist around alcohol use and pregnancy.

Therefore, it is important to raise awareness of FASD.​

PROBLEM

In the past, most anti-alcohol campaigns have used negative emotions

to call for abstinence during pregnancy,

but it will create a mindset of fear and resistance.
